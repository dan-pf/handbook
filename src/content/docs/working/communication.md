Async-first: decisions in written docs, discussion in public channels, DMs only for the personal stuff. Quick question? That's Slack, not email.

## The tools[^practices]

- **Slack** (`planetfitnesscsc.slack.com`) — async messaging within Engineering and across our pods and workstreams.
- **Teams** — async messaging with people outside IT (many don't have a Slack presence), and formal write-ups that need feedback from non-IT collaborators or executive leaders.
- **Email** — last resort: PF executive leaders (who prefer it), internal distribution lists, and external groups.
- **Teams and Zoom** — conferencing; both integrate with Outlook for dial-in info on calendar invites.
- **Confluence** — team documentation: design write-ups, team info, events. Start with the [PF Software Engineering Team](https://planetfitness.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/engg) and [PFX Digital Solutions](https://planetfitness.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/PFX) spaces.
- **Lucid** — our standard diagramming tool (access via `infrasupport@planetcsc.com`).

## Email etiquette

Email is for things that need a record or an external audience.[^etiquette] When you do send one:

- **Clear subject line, clear intent.** The recipient should know from the subject what you need from them.
- **Quick questions don't belong here.** Use Slack/Teams chat for anything that resolves in a message or two.
- **Use the flags when they apply:** Urgent, Low Priority, or Confidential.
- **Sending after hours?** Use Schedule Send, or say explicitly that no immediate response is expected.
- **The Rule of 4:** if an internal thread goes back and forth four times without resolution, stop typing and move to a live conversation.

### Who goes on the email

- **To:** people who need to act, or the core audience.
- **CC:** people who just need to stay informed.
- **BCC:** mass distributions that don't need a response, or when recipient privacy matters.
- Consider addressing team leads instead of entire teams.

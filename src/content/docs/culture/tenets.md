These are the core tenets the engineering team embraces and champions — a combination of what we want to be known for and what we want every new member of the team to know. When we encounter something new, something challenging, or something that causes tension within our team or our organization, the tenets are our guide.

## Culture

- **We aspire to create a [generative culture](https://dora.dev/capabilities/generative-organizational-culture/)** — by adopting practices that reinforce curiosity, feedback, learning, risk-sharing, and creativity.
- **We aspire to create high-trust relationships** with our team members, our partners, and our members — by understanding the dynamics of the [trust equation](https://trustedadvisor.com/why-trust-matters/understanding-trust/understanding-the-trust-equation) and being self-aware enough to change those dynamics when trust is lacking.
- **We aspire to work as a [high-performing team](https://psychsafety.com/googles-project-aristotle/) and continuously improve until we get there** — by providing impactful, meaningful work with structure and clarity, holding ourselves accountable to an environment where excellence and experimentation are expected, and encouraging [team experimentation](https://dora.dev/capabilities/team-experimentation/) within appropriate guardrails.
- **We aspire to be both [empowered in and accountable for](https://www.mindtheproduct.com/empowered-product-teams-by-marty-cagan/) our work** — by understanding the [strategic context](https://www.svpg.com/coaching-strategic-context/) before we begin development, collaborating with Product and Design on [the problems we're trying to solve](https://www.svpg.com/changing-how-you-solve-problems/), and proactively seeking feedback and coaching through transparent, high-trust conversations.
- **We operate as a Judgement Free Zone** — the gym-floor rule applied to the codebase. Questions go in public channels, post-incident reviews are blameless, and code review critiques the code, never the person. Seniority means making it safer for others to be wrong, not being right louder.

## Collaboration

- **We solicit ideas, opinions, and feedback early and often** — especially when our work has ramifications for other people and teams — gathering input during [phased](https://www.svpg.com/product-discovery/) or [continuous discovery](https://www.svpg.com/continuous-discovery/) early enough to actually apply it.
- **We capture our thinking concretely** so others can understand our intent, approach, and decision making — fostering a document-centric culture where [documentation quality](https://dora.dev/capabilities/documentation-quality/) and living documents preserve decisions and, more importantly, their context.

## Communication

- **We proactively share our plans, activities, and challenges** so awareness flows up, down, and across — focusing on [value streams](https://dora.dev/capabilities/work-visibility-in-value-stream/) and sharing the right information with the right people, in the right format.
- **We develop a [learning culture](https://dora.dev/capabilities/learning-culture/)** — internally through [Lunch and Learns](https://planetfitness.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/QE/pages/4746674234), externally through thought leadership, industry engagement, and direct user interactions.

## Discovery

- **We partner with Product Management and Design to address [the four big risks](https://www.svpg.com/four-big-risks/)** — owning feasibility risk and contributing to value, viability, and usability decisions. We identify and prioritize feasibility risks (likelihood × impact), decompose requirements into key questions, and build the simplest, fastest prototypes that answer them.
- **Workstream and pod technical leads act as technical orchestrators** across the organization — creating a Technical Architecture for any new initiative, Technical Designs for any new solution, and Technical FAQs to keep pods and workstreams aligned.

## Delivery

- **We build high-trust relationships and use that trust to unlock the innovations no one else can** — adopting capabilities proven to increase team and organizational performance, and pairing deep user and business context with proximity to the technology to create the "just now possible."
- **We create highly scalable and resilient solutions worthy of our 20M+ members** — through leading practices like [working in small batches](https://dora.dev/capabilities/working-in-small-batches/), [trunk-based development](https://dora.dev/capabilities/trunk-based-development/), [continuous integration](https://dora.dev/capabilities/continuous-integration/), and [continuous delivery](https://dora.dev/capabilities/continuous-delivery/), and through automation: flexible infrastructure, deployment automation, and test automation that covers all four Agile Test Quadrants with a well-shaped test pyramid.

## Operations

- **We own and operate our solutions, in partnership with other teams** — incorporating monitoring and observability into our products, developing proactive failure notifications, and building in security at multiple levels.
- **We build operational maturity by deeply analyzing production issues** — capturing a [Correction of Errors](https://planetfitness.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/QE/pages/4837441592) in the spirit of continuous improvement.
- **We create tooling and documentation sufficient to guide us through unexpected operational scenarios** — preferring automation, and writing runbooks where automation isn't possible so anyone on the team can contribute to incident resolution.
